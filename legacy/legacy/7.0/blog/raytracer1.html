Fixing the Normals


Many of the problems I faced dealt with the normals. I think I had a problem with normals with every single geometry that we were given (square, cube, sphere, triangle).

I think that the blue color is a result from multiplying the normal from object space to world space. As I later learned, this transformation isn't 1:1 like transforming the ray (scale does not preserve the normal). We want to invert the scale and to do this I just multiplied the normal by the Inverse Transpose Matrix. This will preserve the rotation as well as invert the scale.

More Normals...


The sphere is almost there. There's a very small error. Very small. I was stuck on this problem for quite some time (not including the day I didn't work because of a fever).

To see if a ray intersects with a sphere, use of the quadratic formula is needed. There's a tune that accompanies the quadratic formula that goes by "Pop Goes the Weasel" that I learned way back in probably middle school. I'm not going to lie, I would sing the song in my head to remember this formula. But I didn't do it this time for whatever reason. I forgot to divide everything by 2a.

Even the first time when the sphere wouldn't show up, it was an issue with the intersection test. I didn't solve my b correctly. I guess the third time will be a charm.

The lesson learned is: Always check your equation.
