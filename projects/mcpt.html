Integrators

R3 supports three different types of integrators to solve for the Light Transport Equation.
  • Direct Lighting (Light Importance Sampling)
  • Full Lighting (MIS - Light Importance Sampling and BRDF Importance Sampling
  • Full Lighting with Participating Media

    As an extension to the Full Lighting Integrator, R3 can handle scenes with homogenous volumes.
    Ray marching was the primary technique used to solve for the volume in the scene. I detailed more about my implementation on my blog.

Complex Implicit Geometry

WolframAlpha provides a lot of different equations that produce solid geometries.

Ray marching was used to find the intersections of the solids. Given a point on the ray, we can plug in the (x, y, z) values into the equation for the solid. If the equation evaluates to 0, or whatever was specified for the shape, the intersection test returns true and the point gets rendered.
