APIC Fluid Simulator Plugin for Maya

// todo: come up with better name

The final project for CIS660 requires us to find a SIGGRAPH paper to implement as a plugin for Maya or Houdini.
The paper that we opted for was APIC (here's a video), which is essentially the current state of the art in fluid simulation. It improves upon PIC, which dates back to 1963 by Francis Harlow.
Have you seen Moana? The water created for the film utilizes APIC. You can read about it here.

2/13/17

Literature Survey


The literature survey was to guage our attention to the evolution of the paper. How did it get to where it is today?

Before completing this, I was pretty lost in fluid simulations. I remember reading Stam's Stable Fluids paper for the Primer Evaluations but was still confused. After reading and summarizing them, it is a bit clearer. The math is still tricky to understand, but I am sure that'll come in the coming weeks.

Here are the references/seminal work that we traced back from APIC.

  • Harlow, F. 1964. The particle-in-cell method for numerical solution of problems in fluid dynamics. Meth Comp Phys 3, 319--343.
  • Harlow, F., and Welch, E. 1965. Numerical calculation of time dependent viscous flow of fluid with a free surface. Phys Fluid 8, 12, 2182--2189.
  • Brackbill, J., Kothe, D., and Ruppel, H. 1988. Flip: A low-dissipation, pic method for fluid flow. Comp Phys Comm 48, 25--38.
  • Nick Foster , Dimitri Metaxas, Realistic animation of liquids, Graphical Models and Image Processing, v.58 n.5, p.471-483, Sept. 1996
  • Jos Stam, Stable fluids, Proceedings of the 26th annual conference on Computer graphics and interactive techniques, p.121-128, July 1999
  • Yongning Zhu , Robert Bridson, Animating sand as a fluid, ACM Transactions on Graphics (TOG), v.24 n.3, July 2005
