Integrators

Variance Reduction Blog Post

To reduce noise and variance, I calculate the variance for each pixel. If the pixel reaches below a certain threshold, the variance is pretty much negligable and the pixel gets colored; when the variance is above the threshold, the pixel gets recalculated.

One thing that I noticed from doing this, I can do, overall, less samples per pixel which lead to a cleaner render and converges faster. Without this super-sampling method, I would have to do more samples and the resulting render would still have fireflies.

Volumetric Integration Blog Post

This was built upon my Full Lighting Integrator. The entire algorithm to render participating media (volume) requires an extensive amount of ray marching. Basically you ray march from the camera to an object and determine the transmittance. Afterwards, you ray march a second time from the object to the light and determine the in scattering.

Once the ray marches are done, the integrator does MIS and global illumination.

A timeline and explanation of my implementation is available on my blog.
