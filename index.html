Complex Implicit Surfaces

Wolfram has a bunch of equations that represent different shapes. The one that I chose is called the Tanglecube. Basically at each point on the ray, I check to see if the equation equals 0. If it is then we hit an intersection and render that point on the surface. Rather than using a diffuse area light, I used a blue spotlight to get the Tron effect.

900 samples per pixel

8 bounces

  • Date: April 2017
  • Course: Penn CIS561: Advanced Rendering

Typography Projects

After we had linked up and decided that we were all going to live together during the Disney College Program, my roommates and I decided to partake in the DCP tradition of roommate gifts.

I decided to go the homemade route and do some typographical designs. My original plan was to draw my roommates favorite characters dueling it out in a Star Wars scene, but I was running out of time.

Each character consists of different quotes and lyrics from their respective films. Stitch is my favorite character and was the first typography I ever did back in 2011/2012. The difference with that design and the newer typographies is that I used the entire script from the movie as well as all the lyrics.

I definitely like the abstract look more.

  • January 2016

Buzz Lightyear's Space Ranger Spin XP37 Vehicle

This was my first project in 3D Modeling at Penn. It was also the first thing that I was able to model completely, after many years of trying.

During my Disney College Program and working at the Magic Kingdom, I spent a lot of time in Tomorrowland at Buzz Lightyear's Space Ranger Spin. My roommate was the first to max out in points on the ride and got Galactic Hero. The first time I got Galactic Hero in April and challenged to match the amount of times he got Galactic Hero by the time his program ended in May (~20). I didn't meet the goal, BUT by the end of my program (July) I was able to get Galactic Hero 50 times.

TLDR

I opted to design my own variation on the ride vehicle.

  • Date: September 2016
